Primary Research
We spoke to users shortlisted the survey who had actually used OkCupid to understand what their experience really feels like.
This includes chats, calls, in-person interviews
Learnings
After conduction the user interviews via chat, calls, live session, we arrived at some key insights.  We organized these insights into positive and negative affinity cards, and this section presents the key patterns and themes that emerged from that analysis.
Affinity Mapping
We synthesized our interview data by creating an affinity map. This helped us clearly identify recurring patterns, user behaviors, and pain points, turning scattered research data into organized themes.
Where we focused
Poor App Performance & UI
Most user had issue with ui of the app Users were getting confused
Onboarding & Question Relevance
Most user were expiriencing generic and irrelevant questions. Fails to align with user intent.
Low Profile Quality & Trust
This is the most consistent theme. All users who mentioned it had a negative experience.
Understanding the Experience & Pain Points
We synthesized interview data using affinity mapping, grouping observations and quotes from chats, calls, and live sessions. This revealed recurring patterns and key pain points, helping turn scattered insights into clear, actionable themes.
Building the New Journey
After mapping the existing journey, we explored how users would find partners without apps through surveys and conversations.
These real-world insights helped shape a more natural and intuitive user flow.
This insight led us to redesign the journey by making onboarding more relevant, improving clarity in matching, and creating a more progressive and trustworthy experience.
Information Architecture
After gathering the insights from the research, we’ve ideated brainstormed the new IA. The goal was the make the app more trustworthy for the user & improve the onboarding experience.
